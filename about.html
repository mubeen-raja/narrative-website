An introduction to Beyond Earth

Edwin Powell Hubble

Most astronomers by the turn of the twentieth century believed that the universe consisted of only one galaxy, called our Milky Way, described as "an oasis of stars, dust, and gas in the vastness of space". Yet, in 1924, American astronomer named Edwin Hubble studied many other galaxies besides our own Milky Way using the 100-inch Hooker Telescope on Mount Wilson near Los Angeles, California, and discovered that almost all of them were moving away from each other. This suggested that the universe was expanding, which opened a Pandora's box of seminal questions about the universe's possible beginning and end — issues that are still being debated today. (NASA, 2022)
